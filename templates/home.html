The video in the end of the page presents a demonstration of the Benchmarking Framework. The above Controller is the Framework's controller which depicts the components of the framework. The first and mandatory step is the Monitoring. This step trigger the benchmarking engine which will later record the networks' traffic. Second step is the Benchmarking Engine. In this you can select which blockchain network to configure and start, but also you can generate traffic which are transactions between random generated accounts. The managment component is the third one where you can understand the logs of the nodes and the network's information, also you can navigate to Dozzle for the complete logs and to Grafana where you can watch the actual benchmargking diagrams. The Available Networks presents the current running networks configured in previous steps.

About Blockchain Benchmarking Framework

Since the choice of a blockchain protocol is challenging, especially for corporates that seek to use the technology for their products and/or services, we developed an architecture for a blockchain benchmarking framework that aims to serve as a staged environment for supporting blockchain researchers and developers to test and validate the performance of a blockchain protocol under various settings and synthetic scenarios. In addition, the modules provided by the benchmarking framework aim to identify any performance bottlenecks during the network being under heavy load.

Our Team

The Institute for the Future (IFF) is an interdisciplinary research Centre at the University of Nicosia (UNIC), aimed at advancing emerging technologies and contributing to their effective application in industry, government and education as well as evaluating their impact on society at large. IFF’s current focus is on two main areas: (a) Crypto-assets and Blockchains and (b) Forecasting. The Distributed Ledgers Research Centre (DLRC) is hosted at the Institute for the Future (IFF), University of Nicosia (UNIC) and it positions itself as the bridge between pure academic research and the open challenges from the wider Blockchain ecosystem. Our team consists of academic staff, lab researchers and PhD students. We are a diverse mixture of people, fueled by our passion for academic research on blockchains and distributed ledgers. DLRC aims to advance the field with transformative applications of the technology that emerge directly from the ecosystem by turning academic research into applications that can benefit the wider Blockchain ecosystem, by providing computing or storage resources to transactions, redundancy, decentralization and security, and by collaborating with sponsors to support research and technical understanding of protocols. We are proud to offer technical courses design and development programs as the world’s leading University in Blockchain and Digital Currency Education. Our team is fully committed to the Centre’s aims and we are proud to have managed to sign significant agreements with multiple project teams from the ecosystem.

Blockchain is a disruptive technology that focuses on the safe exchange of data between several distributed applications.

Termed by Vitalik Buterin, the blockchain trilemma addresses the challenges developers face in creating a blockchain that is scalable, decentralized and secure — without compromising on any facet.

The motivation for this work is to provide a the necessary tools that aim to study the performance of blockchain protocols and understand how they react under different settings and malicious behaviors.

We are a team of blockchain researchers at an the DLRC lab of University of Nicosia. Exploring the space (at the technical level) we have identified that there is a lack of tools, frameworks and documentation for assessing the performance of blockchain protocols. Most studies available in the literature build simulators. Our approach enables users to deploy close to real scenarios with the use of the actual clients used by a variety of protocols.

Currently our solution supports XRPL protocol, Hyperledger Besu, Geth and Stellar. We are also working on integrating Hyperledger Fabric, Avalanche and IOST blockchains.

Currently, within the Blockchain Benchmarking Framework (BBF) you can work with PoA, PoW, SCP an RPCA.

Along with a blockchain network, the end-user can deploy a monitoring system which is consisted of different services for capturing and visualizing the produced data while a benchmarking test is being executed. Currently, when a blockchain is deployed with the BBF, the user can generate new accounts/wallets, spread the corresponding tokens (i.e. generating traffic in the form of payment transactions) and adapt the network connectivity to create different network topologies. In addition, a benchmarking test could be defined by the user of the BBF within a file, describing a malicious behavior by one or multiple network nodes. An example of such a scenario can be found here.

The first step is to select which blockchain network to configure and start through the Benchmarking Engine. Then, you can easily create wallets and generate traffic in form of payment transactions between random generated accounts. Afterwards, you are able to monitor and visualize all the available information through the Monitoring Engine. Additionally, through the Management page, you have the full control of your network (e.g., start and stop a network). Finally, you can check the Available Networks and always keep up with our latest additions.
